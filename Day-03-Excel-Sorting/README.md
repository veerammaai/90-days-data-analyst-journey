# Day 3 – Excel Sorting & Business Analysis

## 📌 Overview

On Day 3 of my 90-Day Data Analyst Journey, I focused on learning and applying Excel sorting techniques using the Sample Superstore dataset. The objective was 
to organize business data efficiently and answer analytical questions through different sorting methods.

---

## 📊 Dataset Used

* Sample Superstore Dataset (Orders Data)

---

## 🎯 Business Questions Answered

### 1. Which order recorded the highest sales?

**Sorting Applied:** Sales (Largest → Smallest)

**Insight:** Identified the highest-value sales order, along with the corresponding customer, product, and location.

---

### 2. Which products recorded the largest individual losses?

**Sorting Applied:** Profit (Smallest → Largest)

**Insight:** Identified the products associated with the largest individual losses, helping highlight transactions that may require further business investigation.

---

### 3. What are the latest orders in the dataset?

**Sorting Applied:** Order Date (Newest → Oldest)

**Insight:** Identified the most recent customer orders, making it easier to review current business activity.

---

### 4. Which products have the highest sales within each Region and Category?

**Sorting Applied:** Multi-Level Sort (Region → Category → Sales)

**Insight:** Organized the dataset to quickly identify the highest-selling products within each Region and Category combination.

---

## 📈 Key Learnings

* Learned how to perform both single-column and multi-level sorting in Excel.
* Understood when sorting is appropriate for answering business questions.
* Recognized the difference between identifying individual records and calculating grouped totals.
* Improved documentation by recording business questions, techniques, insights, and business value for every analysis.
